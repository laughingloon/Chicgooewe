Sheep

Why sheep? Sheep filled many of my needs:
– ruminant manure and barn bedding for gardens and compost
– my interest in pastures and grazing
– my respect and love of wool, an incredible fiber
– organically raised meat
– moderate size, easy to handle individually and in a flock.
With two acres open and some areas wet, smaller animals made sense.

I bought five Border Leicester ewe lambs (four months old) in July. I had that summer to learn about grazing and handling sheep, a winter feeding hay, and a second summer of grazing. In fall, their second year, they were bred, lambing the next spring. I paid to use a Coopworth ram to service the ewes for the first two breeding years. That way I did not have to keep two separate groups of sheep, rams and ewes.
