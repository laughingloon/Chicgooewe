The Sheep

Why sheep?
Sheep filled many needs:
– ruminant manure and barn bedding for
gardens and compost,
– my interest in pastures and grazing,
– wool is an incredible fiber,
– organically raised meat
– moderate size, easy to handle individually
and in a flock.

With two acres open and some areas wet, smaller animals made sense.

I bought five Border Leicester ewe lambs (four months old) in July. I had summer to learn about grazing and handling them, a winter feeding hay, and a second summer of grazing.

In fall, their second year, they were bred, lambing the next spring. I used a Coopworth ram to service the ewes for the first two breeding years. By paying a stud fee, I did not have to keep and feed a ram for two years.
Border Leicesters have clean faces and legs (no fleece), and Roman noses.
